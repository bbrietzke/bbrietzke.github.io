Not a huge fan of beer, but do like [Celis White](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celis_White).

The only thing is Celis tends to be a little, uhm questionable with business longevity, so
I figure it might be time to make some ( or something close ) for myself.

I don't have much experience home brewing, but there is only one way to get experience!

## Ingredients:
* 2oz Cara Pils Malt
* 2oz Flaked Wheat
* 1lb Wheat Extract DME
* 7g Willamette Hops
* 4g Cascade Hops
* 3g Cracked Coriander
* 3g Orange Zest
* 2gal Clean Water

## Equipment
* 2 gallon pot or vessel
* Cooking Thermometer that can display Fahrenheit
* Grain bag
* three equal sized cups or bowls

## Procedure
Make sure everything is clean and sanitized.  *Everything!*

Add the Cara Pils Malt and the Flaked Wheat to a cheese cloth or grain bag.  

In one of the cups/bowls, add 5g of the Willamette Hops.

In a different bowl, add 2g each of the Cascade and Williamette Hops.

In the last bowl, add 2g of the Cascade Hops and all of the Coriander and Orange Zest.

Bring 1 gallon of water to 155°F in a two gallon pot/vessel.  Once you have temperature, turn off the heat and add the grain bag.  The grain bag should steep for 25 minutes at 155°, so you may have to fiddle
with the heat source to keep it there.  It's okay if it drifts some.

Remove the grain bag from the water and let it drip so most of grainy goodness is
left in the pot.  DO NOT SQUEEZE the grain bag.
